# Glossary of Terms

This glossary defines key terms used throughout the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Textbook.

## A

- **Action Space**: The set of possible actions that a robot can execute in its environment.

- **Affordance**: The relationship between an object's properties and the possible actions that can be performed with it.

- **API (Application Programming Interface)**: A set of rules and protocols for building and interacting with software applications.

- **APA 7**: The 7th edition of the American Psychological Association's publication manual, providing guidelines for academic writing and citation.

- **Autonomous System**: A system that operates independently without human intervention.

## B

- **Behavior Tree**: A hierarchical structure used to organize and execute robot behaviors in a modular fashion.

- **Bipedal Locomotion**: Walking on two legs, typically used to describe humanoid robot movement.

- **Bounding Box**: A rectangular box used in computer vision to define the location and size of an object in an image.

## C

- **Citation**: A reference to a source used in academic writing, following a specific format such as APA.

- **Computer Vision**: A field of artificial intelligence that trains computers to interpret and understand the visual world.

- **Control System**: A system that manages, commands, directs, or regulates the behavior of other devices or systems.

- **Coordinate Frame**: A system of axes used to define positions and orientations in space.

## D

- **Deep Learning**: A subset of machine learning that uses neural networks with multiple layers to model complex patterns.

- **Docker**: A platform that enables developers to package applications and their dependencies into containers.

- **Dynamixel**: A series of smart servo motors used in robotics applications.

## E

- **End-Effector**: The device at the end of a robotic arm that interacts with the environment.

- **Embodied AI**: Artificial intelligence that is integrated into physical systems that interact with the real world.

- **Environment**: The physical or simulated space in which a robot operates.

## F

- **Forward Kinematics**: The process of determining the position and orientation of a robot's end-effector based on joint angles.

- **Fiducial Marker**: A visual marker used for tracking and localization in robotics.

- **Force Control**: A control strategy that regulates the forces applied by a robot to its environment.

## G

- **Gazebo**: A 3D simulation environment used for robotics development and testing.

- **GPU (Graphics Processing Unit)**: A specialized electronic circuit designed to rapidly manipulate and alter memory to accelerate the creation of images in a frame buffer.

- **Grasp Planning**: The process of determining how to securely grasp an object.

- **Ground Truth**: Accurate information that serves as a reference standard for comparing results.

## H

- **Hardware Abstraction Layer**: A layer of software that allows higher-level software to interact with hardware without knowing its specific details.

- **Humanoid Robot**: A robot with human-like form and capabilities.

- **Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)**: The study of interactions between humans and robots.

## I

- **Inverse Kinematics**: The process of determining joint angles required to achieve a desired end-effector position and orientation.

- **Isaac Platform**: NVIDIA's robotics platform for developing AI-powered robots.

- **Inference**: The process of using a trained model to make predictions on new data.

## J

- **Joint Space**: The space defined by the robot's joint angles.

- **Jacobian Matrix**: A matrix that describes the relationship between joint velocities and end-effector velocities.

## K

- **Kinematics**: The study of motion without considering the forces that cause it.

- **Kinetic Chain**: A system of rigid bodies connected by joints to provide controlled motion.

## L

- **Latent Space**: A lower-dimensional space that captures the essential features of high-dimensional data.

- **LIDAR**: Light Detection and Ranging, a remote sensing method that uses light in the form of a pulsed laser to measure distances.

- **Localization**: The process of determining a robot's position and orientation in a known map.

- **Logistic Regression**: A statistical method for binary classification problems.

## M

- **Manipulation**: The ability of a robot to physically interact with objects in its environment.

- **Mapping**: The process of creating a representation of the environment based on sensor data.

- **Middleware**: Software that provides common services and capabilities to applications beyond what's offered by the operating system.

- **Multimodal**: Systems that can process and integrate information from multiple sensory modalities.

## N

- **Navigation**: The ability of a robot to plan and execute paths through its environment.

- **Neural Network**: A computational model inspired by the structure and function of biological neural networks.

- **Node**: A process in ROS that performs computation.

- **Non-holonomic**: A system with constraints on its motion that cannot be integrated to form constraints on configuration.

## O

- **Obstacle Avoidance**: The ability of a robot to navigate around obstacles in its path.

- **Occupancy Grid**: A probabilistic representation of space that indicates the likelihood of occupancy for each cell.

- **OpenCV**: An open-source computer vision and machine learning software library.

- **Operational Space**: The space in which the robot's end-effector operates, typically defined in Cartesian coordinates.

## P

- **Path Planning**: The process of determining a sequence of configurations that moves a robot from start to goal while avoiding obstacles.

- **Perception**: The ability of a robot to interpret sensory information from its environment.

- **PID Controller**: A control loop feedback mechanism widely used in industrial control systems.

- **Point Cloud**: A collection of data points in 3D space, typically generated by 3D scanners or LIDAR sensors.

## Q

- **Q-Learning**: A model-free reinforcement learning algorithm to learn quality of actions.

- **Quaternion**: A mathematical representation of rotation in 3D space that avoids the gimbal lock problem.

## R

- **Real-time**: Systems that must respond to inputs within strict time constraints.

- **Robot Operating System (ROS)**: Flexible framework for writing robot software.

- **ROS 2**: The second generation of the Robot Operating System.

- **Reinforcement Learning**: A type of machine learning where agents learn to take actions in an environment to maximize cumulative reward.

- **Robotics Middleware**: Software that provides common services and capabilities for robotics applications.

## S

- **SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)**: The computational problem of constructing or updating a map of an unknown environment while simultaneously keeping track of an agent's location within it.

- **Sensor Fusion**: The process of combining data from multiple sensors to improve the accuracy and reliability of information.

- **Service**: A ROS communication pattern that allows nodes to send a request and receive a response.

- **State Space**: The set of all possible configurations of a system.

- **System Integration**: The process of bringing together different components to form a unified system.

## T

- **Topic**: A ROS communication channel used for publishing and subscribing to messages.

- **Trajectory**: A time-ordered sequence of positions, velocities, and accelerations for a robot.

- **Transform**: A mathematical representation of the position and orientation of one coordinate frame relative to another.

- **Trust Region**: An optimization method that defines a region around the current iteration within which the model is trusted to be accurate.

## V

- **VLA (Vision-Language-Action)**: Systems that integrate visual perception, language understanding, and physical action.

- **Vision Processing**: The analysis of visual information from cameras or other sensors.

- **Virtual Reality (VR)**: A computer-generated simulation of a three-dimensional environment.

## W

- **Workspace**: The space within which a robot can operate.

- **World Coordinate Frame**: A fixed reference frame used to define positions in a global space.

## Z

- **Zero-Moment Point (ZMP)**: A criterion for static and dynamic stability of humanoid robots.
