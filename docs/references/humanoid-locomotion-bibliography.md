# Humanoid Locomotion Bibliography

This bibliography contains authoritative sources for humanoid robot locomotion, formatted according to APA 7 standards.

## References

1. Kajita, S., Kanehiro, F., Kaneko, K., Fujiwara, K., Harada, K., Yokoi, K., & Hirukawa, H. (2003). Resolved momentum control: Humanoid motion planning based on the linear and angular momentum. *Proceedings of the 2003 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems*, 1644-1650.

2. Kajita, S., Kanehiro, F., Kaneko, K., Yokoi, K., & Hirukawa, H. (2001). The 3D linear inverted pendulum mode: A simple modeling for a biped walking pattern generation. *Proceedings of the 2001 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems*, 239-246.

3. Pratt, J., & Khatib, O. (2002). Impact compensation for bipedal walking robots. *Proceedings of the 2002 IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation*, 2159-2165.

4. Pratt, J., Carff, J., Drakunov, S., & Goswami, A. (2006). Capture point: A step toward humanoid push recovery. *Proceedings of the 2006 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Humanoid Robots*, 200-207.

5. Wight, D. L., Kubica, E. G., & Wang, D. W. (2008). Introduction of the variable-length inverted pendulum for analysis and control of multi-link inverted pendulum models of human walking. *Proceedings of the ASME 2008 International Design Engineering Technical Conferences and Computers and Information in Engineering Conference*, 109-118.

6. Hof, H. K., Gielen, M. G., Van den Berg, J. T., & Duysens, J. (2010). Control of lateral balance in running: Virtual pivot point for ankle moments combined with hip ab/adductions. *Journal of Biomechanics*, 43(5), 978-982.

7. Shih, C. L., & Hsu, S. M. (1992). An approach to biped gait planning using the concept of inverted pendulum. *Robotica*, 10(3), 239-245.

8. Vukobratović, M., & Stepanenko, J. (1972). On the stability of anthropomorphic systems. *Mathematical Biosciences*, 15(1-2), 1-37.

9. Vukobratović, M., & Borovac, B. (2004). Zero-moment point—thirty five years of its life. *International Journal of Humanoid Robotics*, 1(1), 157-173.

10. Kuo, A. D. (2007). The six determinants of gait and internal model evaluation of a bipedal walking control. *Journal of Biomechanics*, 40(6), 1343-1350.

11. Hur, P., Audren, H., Hsu, A., & Gregg, R. D. (2014). A unified approach to three-dimensional gait generation for low- and high-functioning paraplegic patients. *IEEE Transactions on Neural Systems and Rehabilitation Engineering*, 23(2), 267-277.

12. Sardain, P., & Bessonnet, G. (2004). Forces acting on a biped robot. Center of pressure-zero moment point. *IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics-Part A: Systems and Humans*, 34(6), 630-637.
