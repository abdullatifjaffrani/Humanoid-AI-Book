# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

This page addresses common questions about the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Textbook.

## General Questions

### Q: What is Physical AI?
A: Physical AI refers to artificial intelligence systems that interact with the physical world through robotic embodiment. It combines perception, reasoning, and action in real-world environments.

### Q: Who is this textbook for?
A: This textbook is designed for undergraduate and graduate students, instructors, and lab engineers working with ROS 2, simulation environments, NVIDIA Isaac platform, and Vision-Language-Action systems.

### Q: What prerequisites do I need?
A: Basic programming knowledge (Python/C++), understanding of Linux command line, and familiarity with fundamental robotics concepts.

## Technical Questions

### Q: What hardware do I need?
A: For full experience: Ubuntu 22.04, ROS 2 Humble, NVIDIA GPU with CUDA support. For basic content: Any modern computer with internet access.

### Q: How long does the curriculum take?
A: The curriculum is designed for 14 weeks with 3-5 hours per week depending on background and depth of exploration.

### Q: Can I use this for self-study?
A: Yes! The textbook is designed for both formal education and self-paced learning.

## Software Setup

### Q: Which ROS 2 distribution should I use?
A: ROS 2 Humble Hawksbill is recommended as it's an LTS version with long-term support.

### Q: Do I need a robot to follow this curriculum?
A: No, most content can be completed with simulation environments. Physical robots are optional for advanced exercises.

## Getting Help

### Q: Where can I get help if stuck?
A: Check the troubleshooting section, search the community forums, or reach out to your instructor if taking this as a course.

### Q: How do I contribute improvements?
A: See the contributing guidelines in the repository or contact the authors directly.

## About the Capstone Project

### Q: What is the capstone project?
A: The capstone project integrates all concepts in a complete autonomous humanoid robot system combining ROS 2, simulation, Isaac platform, and VLA systems.

### Q: How difficult is the capstone project?
A: The capstone is challenging but achievable, designed to demonstrate mastery of all concepts covered in weeks 1-14.
